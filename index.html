Don Norman – Key Biography

Donald Arthur Norman (b. 1935) is a pioneer in Human–Computer Interaction (HCI), known for user-centered design and usability engineering.

Education & Career

  • B.S. Electrical Engineering, MIT; M.S. & Ph.D. Psychology, University of Pennsylvania
  • Faculty: UCSD, Harvard, Northwestern, KAIST
  • Industry: VP at Apple, HP executive, IDEO Fellow, co-founder of Nielsen Norman Group

Contributions

  • Popularized User-Centered Design (UCD) / Human-Centered Design (HCD)
  • Shaped modern User Experience (UX)
  • Books: The Design of Everyday Things, Emotional Design, Living with Complexity

Awards

  • Benjamin Franklin Medal (Computer & Cognitive Science)
  • Member, National Academy of Engineering
  • Fellow, American Academy of Arts and Sciences

GOMS Creators – Brief Biographies

Stuart K. Card

American HCI pioneer and co-author of The Psychology of Human-Computer Interaction (1983), where GOMS was first described. Senior Research Fellow at Xerox PARC.

Thomas P. Moran

American computer scientist and HCI researcher at Xerox PARC. Co-founded the journal Human–Computer Interaction.

Allen Newell (1927–1992)

American cognitive scientist and AI pioneer. Co-founder of CMU’s AI Lab and Turing Award winner (1975).
